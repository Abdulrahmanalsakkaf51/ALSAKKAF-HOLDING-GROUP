# 1. Purpose

The Partner Operating Model defines how ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP uses Partners inside AOS.

At the beginning stage, ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP may not yet have employees, offices, revenue, or a physical headquarters.

However, it can still operate with structure by using:

- The Founder
- AOS
- Documentation
- Knowledge systems
- AI Partners
- Automation tools
- Clear decision rules
- GitHub version control

This model allows the institution to begin operating before it becomes a fully staffed physical company.

---

# 2. Current Company Stage

ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP is currently in the **Founder-Led Partner-Powered Stage**.

This means:

- The Founder provides vision, judgment, responsibility, and approval.
- AOS provides structure, memory, and operating discipline.
- Partners support research, writing, analysis, planning, documentation, and future automation.
- Human accountability remains with the Founder / CEO.

Partners do not replace leadership.

Partners extend the Founder’s capacity.

---

# 3. What Is a Partner?

A Partner is an AI system, automation workflow, digital assistant, or intelligent tool that supports collaborators and leadership inside AOS.

Partners may help with:

- Research
- Documentation
- Analysis
- Planning
- Drafting
- Searching knowledge
- Summarizing records
- Creating templates
- Supporting decisions
- Monitoring information
- Automating repeatable work

Partners are not employees.

Partners are not legal officers.

Partners are not owners.

Partners are support systems inside AOS.

---

# 4. Partner Philosophy

Partners exist to strengthen the institution.

A Partner should help ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP become:

- More organized
- More informed
- More consistent
- More productive
- More documented
- More intelligent
- More scalable

Partners should not make the company careless.

Partners should make the company more responsible.

---

# 5. Human Accountability

Human accountability is mandatory.

Partners may assist, recommend, summarize, draft, or analyze.

However:

- Partners do not approve major decisions.
- Partners do not replace the CEO.
- Partners do not replace legal, financial, or professional responsibility.
- Partners do not own institutional judgment.
- Partners do not act outside approved boundaries.

Final responsibility remains with human leadership.

---

# 6. Partner Authority Levels

AOS defines Partner authority levels.

| Level | Name | Meaning |
|------|------|---------|
| Level 0 | Reference | Partner provides information only |
| Level 1 | Assist | Partner helps with drafting, summarizing, or organizing |
| Level 2 | Recommend | Partner suggests options for human review |
| Level 3 | Prepare | Partner prepares documents, plans, or workflows for approval |
| Level 4 | Execute With Approval | Partner supports execution only after human approval |
| Level 5 | Autonomous Execution | Not allowed at the foundation stage |

At the current stage, Partners may operate only between **Level 0 and Level 3**.

Level 4 may be allowed later for low-risk workflows after governance is created.

Level 5 is not approved.

# 8. First Partner Priority

The first official Partner should be:

## The Librarian

The Librarian is the front desk of institutional knowledge.

Its purpose is to help the Founder, collaborators, and future Partners find approved knowledge inside AOS.

The Librarian may answer questions such as:

- Where is The Mind defined?
- Which document explains the AOS Lifecycle?
- Which ADR approved the Knowledge System?
- What lesson teaches the Growth Model?
- Where is the Partner Operating Model?
- Which document should I update for this decision?

The Librarian should be built before more advanced Partners because all future Partners need reliable knowledge access.

---

# 9. Partner Lifecycle

Every official Partner should follow a lifecycle.

```text
Need Identified
  ↓
Partner Purpose Defined
  ↓
Knowledge Sources Identified
  ↓
Operating Rules Written
  ↓
Prompt / Workflow Created
  ↓
Testing
  ↓
Approval
  ↓
Partner Registry Entry
  ↓
Use
  ↓
Monitoring
  ↓
Improvement
```

This prevents random AI usage and protects institutional quality.

---

# 10. Partner Requirements

Before a Partner becomes official, it must have:

1. A clear name
2. A clear purpose
3. A defined owner
4. Approved knowledge sources
5. Allowed tasks
6. Restricted tasks
7. Human approval rules
8. A testing method
9. A Partner Registry entry
10. A review cycle

---

# 11. Partner Registry

The Partner Registry is the official list of approved, proposed, and retired Partners.

It should include:

- Partner name
- Purpose
- Status
- Owner
- Authority level
- Knowledge sources
- Related documents
- Review date

No Partner should be considered official until it appears in the Partner Registry.

---

# 12. Partner Boundaries

Partners must not:

- Make final strategic decisions alone
- Approve spending alone
- Sign contracts
- Provide final legal judgment
- Provide final medical judgment
- Misrepresent themselves as humans
- Access sensitive data without approval
- Operate outside their documented role
- Replace human accountability

Partners must remain inside AOS governance.

---

# 13. Partner Knowledge Sources

Partners should use approved institutional knowledge whenever possible.

Approved sources may include:

- Company Constitution
- Digital DNA
- Company Language Standard
- Knowledge Register
- Architecture Decision Log
- ADR files
- AOS University lessons
- SOPs
- Project records
- Reports
- Partner Registry

Partners become stronger when the institution’s knowledge is organized.

---

# 14. Partner Quality Control

Partner outputs must be reviewed.

Quality control may include checking:

- Accuracy
- Alignment with AOS
- Alignment with the Constitution
- Clarity
- Completeness
- Risk
- Bias
- Missing assumptions
- Source reliability
- Human approval needs

A Partner output is not automatically true just because it sounds confident.

---

# 15. Partner Review Cycle

Partners should be reviewed regularly.

| Review Type | Frequency | Purpose |
|------------|-----------|---------|
| Quick Review | Monthly | Check usefulness and problems |
| Performance Review | Quarterly | Evaluate quality and reliability |
| Architecture Review | When changed | Check fit with AOS |
| Retirement Review | When no longer useful | Decide whether to retire or replace |

---

---

# 17. Future Collaborators

When human collaborators join the institution, Partners should support them.

Partners may help collaborators:

- Learn faster
- Find knowledge
- Draft documents
- Understand systems
- Follow SOPs
- Manage projects
- Analyze information
- Reduce repetitive work

Partners should make collaborators stronger, not less important.

---

# 18. Partner Operating Principle

> Partners extend human capacity, but they do not replace human responsibility.

---

# 19. Partner Operating Rule

No Partner becomes official inside ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP until its purpose, authority level, knowledge sources, restrictions, and review process are documented.
