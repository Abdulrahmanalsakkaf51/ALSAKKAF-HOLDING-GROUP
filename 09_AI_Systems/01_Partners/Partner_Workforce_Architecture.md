# 1. Purpose

The Partner Workforce Architecture defines how Partners are organized inside ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP and its future companies.

This document explains how a company can operate with Partners in structured roles such as:

- Worker Partners
- Supervisor Partners
- Manager Partners
- Company Lead Partners
- Reporting Partners
- Knowledge Partners
- Holding-level Partners

The purpose is to prevent random AI usage and create a structured Partner-powered company model.

---

# 2. Core Idea

ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP is designed to become a Partner-powered holding company.

Each company under the holding group may eventually operate with a structured Partner workforce.

A future company may have around **30 Partners**, but the number is not the goal.

The goal is structure.

A company should only add Partners when the work, roles, rules, and reporting lines are clear.

---

# 3. Human Accountability

Partners may support work, supervision, reporting, planning, and preparation.

However, Partners do not replace human accountability.

At the foundation stage:

- The Founder / CEO remains the final authority.
- Partners cannot make final strategic decisions alone.
- Partners cannot approve spending alone.
- Partners cannot sign contracts.
- Partners cannot replace legal, financial, or professional judgment.
- Partners must operate inside AOS governance.

---

# 4. Partner Workforce Layers

A Partner-powered company uses layers.

```text
Founder / CEO
    ↓
Holding-Level Partners
    ↓
Company Lead Partner
    ↓
Manager Partners
    ↓
Supervisor Partners
    ↓
Worker Partners
    ↓
Reporting + Knowledge Capture
    ↓
AOS Improves
```

Each layer has a different responsibility.

---

# 5. Founder / CEO Layer

The Founder / CEO provides:

- Vision
- Final approval
- Strategic direction
- Ethical judgment
- Accountability
- Resource decisions
- Company-level priorities

Partners support the Founder, but they do not replace the Founder.

---

# 6. Holding-Level Partners

Holding-level Partners support ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP across all companies.

Examples include:

| Partner | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Founder Support Partner | Helps the Founder plan, prioritize, and prepare decisions |
| Strategy Partner | Supports strategic analysis and direction |
| Finance Partner | Supports budgets, financial tracking, and models |
| Risk Partner | Identifies risks, assumptions, and missing information |
| Technology Partner | Supports software, automation, and technical architecture |
| The Librarian | Finds and explains institutional knowledge |
| Project Partner | Supports project planning and tracking |
| Reporting Partner | Prepares summaries, reports, and performance snapshots |

Holding-level Partners help the whole institution, not just one company.

---

# 7. Company Lead Partner

Each future company may have one Company Lead Partner.

The Company Lead Partner supports the overall management of a specific business under ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP.

Its purpose is to:

- Understand the company’s mission
- Coordinate Manager Partners
- Prepare company updates
- Track company goals
- Identify risks and blockers
- Escalate decisions to the Founder / CEO
- Ensure the company follows AOS

The Company Lead Partner does not replace a human CEO.

It prepares information and coordination for human approval.

---

# 8. Manager Partners

Manager Partners support major business functions.

A company may have Manager Partners such as:

| Manager Partner | Purpose |
|----------------|---------|
| Operations Manager Partner | Supports workflows, SOPs, execution, and internal operations |
| Marketing Manager Partner | Supports campaigns, content, branding, and growth |
| Sales / Customer Manager Partner | Supports leads, inquiries, customer journeys, and service |
| Finance / Admin Manager Partner | Supports budgets, invoices, cost tracking, and admin records |
| Product / Service Manager Partner | Supports the company’s offer, service delivery, and improvement |

Manager Partners coordinate workstreams.

They do not make final human decisions.

---

# 9. Supervisor Partners

Supervisor Partners support smaller work areas under Manager Partners.

They help organize work, check quality, and prepare updates.

Examples:

- Content Supervisor Partner
- Campaign Supervisor Partner
- Customer Inquiry Supervisor Partner
- Listing Supervisor Partner
- Research Supervisor Partner
- Service Quality Supervisor Partner
- Data Supervisor Partner
- Workflow Supervisor Partner

Supervisor Partners help make sure work is organized and reviewed before it reaches higher levels.

---

# 10. Worker Partners

Worker Partners perform specific focused tasks.

Examples:

- Script Partner
- Design Partner
- Editing Partner
- Research Partner
- Listing Partner
- Outreach Partner
- Data Entry Partner
- SEO Partner
- Customer FAQ Partner
- Report Drafting Partner
- Market Research Partner
- Property Research Partner

Worker Partners should have narrow, clear responsibilities.

The narrower the responsibility, the easier it is to test, improve, and control quality.

---

# 11. Reporting Partner

Each company should have a Reporting Partner.

The Reporting Partner prepares:

- Daily updates
- Weekly summaries
- Project status reports
- Performance snapshots
- Risk summaries
- Lessons learned
- Pending decisions

The Reporting Partner helps the Founder / CEO see what is happening without needing to manually inspect every task.

---

# 12. Knowledge Partner

Each company should have a Knowledge Partner or a connection to The Librarian.

The Knowledge Partner helps capture:

- Lessons learned
- Useful prompts
- SOP improvements
- Customer insights
- Market insights
- Mistakes
- Successful methods
- Repeatable workflows

This ensures that company activity strengthens AOS.

---

# 13. Standard 30-Partner Company Cell

A future company may use a 30-Partner structure.

This is called a **30-Partner Company Cell**.

| Layer | Number | Role |
|------|--------|------|
| Company Leadership | 1 | Company Lead Partner |
| Management | 5 | Manager Partners |
| Supervision | 5 | Supervisor Partners |
| Execution | 15 | Worker Partners |
| Specialist Support | 2 | Risk / Customer / Data / Quality Partners |
| Reporting | 1 | Reporting Partner |
| Knowledge | 1 | Knowledge Partner |

Total:

```text
1 + 5 + 5 + 15 + 2 + 1 + 1 = 30 Partners
```

This structure is a target model, not a starting requirement.

A new company may begin with only 3 to 5 Partners and grow gradually.

# 17. Partner Role Design Requirements

Every Partner role must define:

1. Partner name
2. Partner ID
3. Company or ecosystem
4. Purpose
5. Role type
6. Authority level
7. Inputs
8. Outputs
9. Allowed tasks
10. Restricted tasks
11. Knowledge sources
12. Reporting line
13. Review method
14. Human approval requirements

---

# 18. Partner Scaling Rule

Partners should be added gradually.

A company should not start with 30 Partners immediately.

A safer path is:

```text
3 Partners
  ↓
5 Partners
  ↓
10 Partners
  ↓
15 Partners
  ↓
30 Partners
```

Each stage should be tested before moving to the next.

---

# 19. Partner Quality Control

Partner work must be reviewed.

Quality control should check:

- Accuracy
- Usefulness
- Alignment with AOS
- Alignment with the company goal
- Missing assumptions
- Risk
- Repetition
- Clarity
- Human approval needs
- Whether knowledge should be captured

No Partner output should be treated as final without review.

---

# 20. Partner Workforce Principle

> A Partner workforce becomes powerful when every Partner has a clear role, clear boundary, clear reporting line, and clear relationship to AOS.

---

# 21. Partner Workforce Rule

No company under ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP should scale its Partner workforce until roles, authority levels, reporting lines, knowledge sources, and quality controls are documented.
