# 3. Problem This Partner Solves

AOS, Atlas, The Librarian, and every current and future company cell hold growing amounts of institutional knowledge, credentials-adjacent workflows, client work, files, dashboards, marketing systems, and future integrations, with no dedicated Partner responsible for cybersecurity governance and digital trust.

No existing Partner in the Partner Registry (PREG-001) covers this purpose. The closest related entries are PARTNER-011 Risk Partner (general risk/assumption identification, not security-specific) and PARTNER-009 Technology Partner (software/architecture planning, not security governance). Neither is built, and neither is scoped specifically to cybersecurity policy, safe tool review, incident documentation, and digital trust controls.

The `aos-guardian-cybersecurity` Claude skill (CSKILL-011) already exists as planning-only support for this gap. This request turns that planning capability into a formally documented Partner.

# 6. Proposed Role

Cybersecurity & Digital Trust Partner — protects AOS, Atlas, Partners, company knowledge, client work, files, credentials, dashboards, marketing systems, and future integrations through defensive cybersecurity governance, risk review, safe testing plans, security checklists, incident documentation, and digital trust controls.

# 7. Company or Department Served

AOS, Atlas, The Librarian, and all future company cells under ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP.

# 8. Expected Tasks

| Task | Frequency | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Review a new tool, vendor, or automation for security/data risk | On demand | Documentation review only — no live testing of the tool |
| Draft or update cybersecurity policy and checklist documents | On demand | Feeds Knowledge Register once approved |
| Review a proposed Partner's data access rules for sensitivity concerns | On demand | Supports Partner Factory activation gate (PCL-001 Stage 3.6) |
| Prepare incident response and incident log templates | On demand | Documentation only, no live incident handling authority |
| Recommend approval gates before connecting email, calendar, or external tools | On demand | Advisory to Founder/Atlas |
| Prepare security awareness material | Occasional | For Founder and future collaborators |
| Escalate high-risk findings to the Founder | As needed | Guardian cannot approve or resolve high-risk items itself |

---

# 9. Data Needed

Guardian would need to read: the Partner Registry, Partner Profiles, Partner Factory templates and lifecycle documents, the Knowledge Register, AOS architecture documents (ADDA-001, ASAA-001, APFA-001), and any tool/vendor documentation submitted for review.

Guardian must never require or receive: credentials, API keys, passwords, private student data, or private client data. Where a review touches such data, Guardian works from a description of the data category, not the data itself.

---

# 10. Sensitivity Level

| Level | Applies |
|-------|---------|
| Public / Internal | Yes |
| Confidential | Yes (policy and risk review content) |
| Restricted (credentials, private student or client data) | No — Guardian must never access this data directly |

---

# 11. Local/Online Behavior

Guardian is expected to operate local-first: reading existing AOS Markdown documents and drafting policy/checklist/review output using local reasoning. Occasional online AI use (Claude sessions) follows ADDA-001 routing rules. Restricted data must never be sent to an online AI service, and Guardian has no restricted-data access to begin with.

---

# 12. Estimated Cost Behavior

Expected model tier: local tool / documentation review first, cheap-to-standard online model (Claude session) for drafting policy and review documents. No paid API usage, no recurring subscription cost expected at this stage. Any future paid security tool would require a separate Partner_Budget_Approval_Template.md submission.
