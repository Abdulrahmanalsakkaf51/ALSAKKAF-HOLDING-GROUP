# Important Rule

**No product may be listed or advertised before CEO approval. No counterfeit, misleading, unsafe, restricted, or IP-infringing products. No ad spend without budget approval.**

This rule applies to every section below without exception.

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines how ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP runs dropshipping and marketplace selling as a small, controlled experiment — with clear research steps, approval gates, risk warnings, and decision gates — so it never becomes an uncontrolled or unsafe activity.

---

# 2. Why Dropshipping Is an Experiment, Not the First Cash Engine

| Factor | AI Services (First Cash Engine) | Dropshipping (Experiment) |
|--------|----------------------------------|----------------------------|
| Time to first cash | Days, once an offer and a client exist | Weeks to months, requires product/supplier/store validation |
| Margin | Typically high (labor/expertise-based) | Typically thin after platform fees, ad spend, and shipping |
| Legal/logistics complexity | Low (documents, advice, digital delivery) | Higher (customs, refunds, IP risk, marketplace policy risk) |
| Validation cost | Low (a proposal and a conversation) | Requires real spend to truly test demand |
| Control | Fully controlled by AOS/Founder skill | Partly controlled by suppliers and marketplaces |

Per `AOS_Revenue_Launch_Master_Plan.md` (STRAT-005), AI Services is the first cash engine. Dropshipping runs as a later, small, budget-capped experiment — never the source of first-month revenue.

---

# 3. Product Research Workflow

Research is manual and documentation-based only. No scraping, automated data collection, or unauthorized tools are used.

1. Identify a candidate product category from public marketplace browsing, public trend pages, or manual competitor review.
2. Record the candidate in the Product Test Template (`Dropshipping_Product_Test_Template.md`, RLT-011).
3. Check public demand signals manually (marketplace bestseller/search visibility, general search interest).
4. Check public competition level (how many sellers, how saturated the listing category looks).
5. Estimate cost, shipping, and fees using publicly available supplier/platform information.
6. Bring the candidate to the CEO for a Product Validation Criteria review (Section 8) before any further step.

---

# 4. Supplier Review Checklist

| Check | What to Verify |
|-------|-----------------|
| Reliability | Supplier has a track record, verifiable reviews, or a known platform presence |
| Shipping time | Realistic, disclosed shipping time; avoid suppliers with excessive or undisclosed delays |
| Return policy | Supplier has a clear, workable return/replacement policy |
| Communication | Supplier responds clearly and promptly to sample questions |
| Reviews | Independent buyer reviews exist and are not uniformly suspicious/fake-looking |
| Red flags | No pricing that looks too good to be true, no refusal to disclose origin, no pressure tactics, no missing business information |

No supplier is engaged for a real order until this checklist is completed and reviewed.

# 6. UAE/GCC Marketplace Notes

- Any real trading activity should be reviewed against UAE commercial and tax obligations (including general VAT registration-threshold awareness) before it becomes a real, ongoing business — this document does not substitute for legal or accounting advice.
- UAE consumer protection expectations (accurate listings, honest pricing, working returns) apply to any real storefront.
- Recommend a short legal/accounting review before any UAE-based real trading begins, not just before scaling.

---

# 7. US/International Experiment Notes

- Cross-border shipping introduces customs, duties, and delivery-time uncertainty that must be disclosed to buyers.
- Currency conversion affects margin calculations — always calculate profit in AED after conversion.
- Each platform (eBay, Amazon) has its own seller eligibility, verification, and payout requirements that must be completed before listing — see platform checklists below.

# 8. eBay Experiment Checklist

1. CEO manually creates and verifies the eBay seller account (see `AOS_Channel_and_Account_Setup_Runbook.md`, STRAT-009).
2. Complete seller verification and payout setup.
3. Confirm the candidate product passes Product Validation Criteria (Section 10).
4. Draft the listing (title, description, images, price, shipping policy, return policy) for CEO review.
5. CEO approves the listing before it goes live.
6. Track first-order performance against the Profit Calculator (Section 13).

# 9. Amazon Seller Checklist

1. CEO manually creates and verifies the Amazon seller account.
2. Review Amazon's category approval and restricted-product rules for the candidate product.
3. Confirm the candidate product passes Product Validation Criteria (Section 10).
4. Draft the listing for CEO review, including compliant images and accurate claims.
5. CEO approves the listing before it goes live.

# 11. Product Validation Criteria

| Criterion | Question | Pass Signal |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Demand signal | Is there visible, ongoing public demand for this product? | Yes, with evidence recorded |
| Competition | Is the category saturated beyond a realistic entry point? | Moderate competition, room to differentiate |
| Margin | After cost, shipping, and fees, is margin meaningfully positive? | Estimated margin comfortably above break-even |
| Shipping feasibility | Can shipping time/cost be disclosed honestly and still be acceptable to buyers? | Yes |
| Return risk | Is the product low-risk for damage, wrong-fit, or dissatisfaction returns? | Low-to-moderate return risk |
| Brand fit | Is the product safe, legal, and consistent with ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP's reputation? | Yes |
| IP/counterfeit check | Is the product free of counterfeit or IP-infringement risk? | Confirmed "No risk identified" |

A product must pass every criterion, and receive explicit CEO approval, before any listing or ad is created.

# 12. Ad Budget Cap

- Default ad budget for any dropshipping experiment: **AED 0**.
- Any test ad spend requires a specific CEO-approved amount, tied to the overall expense cap in `AOS_Revenue_Launch_Master_Plan.md` (STRAT-005): AED 1,000–6,000 total for the first 30 days across the entire Revenue Launch package, not per product.
- No ad is created or run before CEO budget approval, per CLAUDE.md Section 4.

---

# 13. Refund/Return Risk

- Every listing must state an honest, workable return policy before it goes live.
- Maintain a small reserve (tracked in the Profit Calculator) for expected returns/refunds.
- Respond to refund requests promptly and fairly; slow or unfair handling drives platform penalties and reputational damage.
- If return rate exceeds the Kill gate threshold (Section 15), the product is paused or killed regardless of otherwise positive sales.

---

# 14. IP/Counterfeit Warning

No counterfeit, misleading, unsafe, restricted, or IP-infringing products may ever be sourced, listed, or advertised. This includes:

- Branded replicas or unlicensed use of trademarks/logos
- Products making unverified medical, safety, or performance claims
- Products restricted or banned on the target marketplace or in the destination country
- Products with unclear or unverifiable origin/safety documentation

Any doubt about a product's legitimacy stops the process immediately and is escalated to the CEO — no listing proceeds until resolved.

---

# 15. Customer Service Workflow

1. Monitor order and message queues daily once any real listing is live.
2. Respond to inquiries within one business day.
3. For complaints: acknowledge promptly, verify the issue, offer the stated return/refund remedy.
4. Log every complaint and resolution (date, issue, resolution, outcome).
5. Escalate any pattern of complaints (3+ on the same issue) to the CEO for a product-level decision gate review (Section 16).

# 16. Profit Calculator Fields

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Product cost | Supplier unit cost |
| Shipping cost | Cost to ship to buyer |
| Platform fee | Marketplace commission/listing fee |
| Payment processing fee | Payment gateway/processor fee |
| Ad spend | Allocated ad cost per unit sold (if any, CEO-approved only) |
| Return rate estimate | Expected % of orders refunded/returned |
| Net margin | Selling price minus all costs above, adjusted for return rate |

Use `Dropshipping_Product_Test_Template.md` (RLT-011) and `Revenue_Dashboard_Data_Template.md` (RLT-012) to record these fields per product.

---

# 17. Decision Gates

| Gate | Trigger |
|------|---------|
| Test | Product passes Product Validation Criteria (Section 11) and receives CEO approval; listed with default AED 0 ad spend |
| Pause | Fewer than 3 orders after 2 weeks live, or return rate exceeds 15% in the test window — pause listing, review, do not delete data |
| Kill | Net margin is negative across 5+ orders, or any IP/counterfeit/safety issue is identified, or return rate exceeds 25% — remove listing, record lessons learned, do not resource further |
| Scale | 10+ consistent orders with positive net margin sustained over 3+ weeks, and CEO approves an increased, capped ad budget — only then may spend increase beyond the default AED 0 |

No gate transition skips CEO approval. Every decision is logged in the project record's progress log.
