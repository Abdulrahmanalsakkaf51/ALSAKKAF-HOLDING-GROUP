# 1. Purpose

This document defines how ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP finds, qualifies, sells to, and delivers work for AI Services clients, using Atlas and Partners inside AOS.

The governing principle is fixed:

> Atlas may prepare, coordinate, and operate approved workflows. CEO approval is required before final price, final proposal, contract/deal acceptance, large spending, sensitive data access, or risky outreach.

This document does not authorize any live outreach, pricing, or contract activity. It defines the model those activities must follow once approved.

---

# 2. Atlas Lead Research Model

Atlas may, without per-instance approval:

- research publicly available information about potential client companies and industries,
- build long-lists of candidate leads from public sources,
- organize findings into structured lead profiles (company, sector, apparent need, public contact channel),
- flag which leads best match the AI Services offer defined in STRAT-001.

Atlas may not, without approval:

- purchase any paid data, list, or tool to support research,
- access any private, gated, or sensitive data source,
- contact any lead directly.

Output of this stage: a **Lead Long-List**, stored for CEO review before any lead advances.

---

# 3. Partner Task Assignment Model

Once the CEO reviews the Lead Long-List, approved leads move into task-based work assigned to Partners:

| Task | Typical Owner | Approval Needed to Start |
|------|----------------|---------------------------|
| Deeper research on an approved lead | Partner | No (lead already approved) |
| Drafting an outreach message | Partner | No (draft only) |
| Sending outreach | Partner (execution) | Yes — Outreach Approval Gate (Section 5) |
| Drafting a proposal | Partner | No (draft only) |
| Finalizing a proposal or price | Partner (draft) / CEO (final) | Yes — CEO final approval |
| Delivering approved client work | Partner | No, once contract is signed and scope is approved |

Partners work strictly inside the scope of the task assigned. A Partner drafting an outreach message may not also decide to send it — drafting and sending are separated by the approval gate in Section 5.

---

# 4. Lead Qualification Model

Every lead on the Lead Long-List is scored before advancing, using a simple qualification table:

| Criterion | Question | Weight |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| Fit | Does the lead's apparent need match an AI Services offer we can deliver today? | High |
| Reachability | Is there a legitimate, public, non-intrusive way to reach them? | High |
| Signal | Is there a visible trigger (hiring, public complaint, outdated systems, growth signal) suggesting timing is right? | Medium |
| Size | Is the lead's apparent size consistent with our Month 1–3 delivery capacity (Section 8)? | Medium |
| Risk | Is there anything (reputation, sector sensitivity, regulatory complexity) that raises delivery or compliance risk? | Must be reviewed |

Output of this stage: a **Qualified Lead Table** with a fit score and a one-line rationale per lead, submitted for CEO review before outreach drafting begins.

---

# 5. Outreach Approval Gate

No outreach message reaches a lead without explicit CEO approval of that specific message and recipient.

Process:

1. Partner drafts outreach message referencing the qualified lead.
2. Draft is placed in front of the CEO with: lead name, qualification summary, proposed channel (e.g., email, LinkedIn), and the exact message text.
3. CEO approves, edits, or rejects.
4. Only after explicit approval does the message get sent, and sending is a logged action (Section 10).

This gate cannot be bypassed by volume (e.g., batching many leads into one approval request) unless the CEO explicitly approves a batch process in advance.

---

# 6. Sales Report Format

Atlas maintains a standing sales report so the CEO always has current pipeline visibility. Minimum fields:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Lead / Client Name | Who |
| Stage | Long-List / Qualified / Outreach Sent / In Conversation / Proposal Drafted / Proposal Approved / Contract Sent / Won / Lost |
| Last Action | What happened most recently |
| Next Action | What is proposed next, and who owns it |
| Value Estimate | Cautious estimate of deal size, if known |
| Approval Needed | Yes/No — flags anything currently waiting on the CEO |

This report is descriptive, not decisional — it informs the CEO, it does not authorize any stage transition by itself.

# 7. Pricing Approval Rule

- Partners may draft price ranges for internal discussion, based on the cost/effort of the proposed work and comparable offers.
- No price is final, and no price may be shown to a client, until the CEO explicitly approves that specific price for that specific client.
- Any discount, custom package, or payment-term deviation from a previously approved price also requires separate CEO approval before being offered.

---

# 8. Deal Closing Rule

- No contract, proposal, or verbal commitment is binding on ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP unless the CEO has explicitly approved the final terms.
- Partners may negotiate scope clarifications with a client, but may not agree to final scope, price, timeline, or legal terms on the company's behalf.
- Once the CEO approves a deal, it moves to Client Delivery Workflow (Section 9) and is logged (Section 10).

---

# 9. Client Delivery Workflow

1. **Kickoff** — confirm scope, deliverables, timeline, and point of contact against the approved proposal.
2. **Assignment** — CEO or Atlas assigns Partners to delivery tasks, each scoped narrowly (consistent with Section 3).
3. **Build** — Partners execute; Atlas tracks status against the Sales Report / delivery equivalent.
4. **Internal Review** — work is checked against the approved scope before anything client-facing goes out.
5. **Client Check-in** — status shared with the client at agreed intervals; any scope change routes back through Pricing Approval (Section 7) if it affects price or effort.
6. **Delivery** — final output handed over per contract terms.
7. **Close-out** — outcome logged (Section 10) and fed into the Client Success Loop (Section 11).

---

# 10. Compliance Rule

- No client data is stored, processed, or shared outside approved, access-controlled locations.
- No sensitive data (financial, personal, credentials) is accessed by a Partner without a specific CEO approval for that data and that purpose.
- No client work may be publicized (case study, YouTube content, portfolio use) without the client's explicit consent and separate CEO approval, consistent with STRAT-001 Section 11.
- All client commitments must be consistent with whatever legal/company structure is active at the time (see STRAT-001 Section 9, Compliance Risk).

---

# 11. Audit Log Rule

Every approval-gated action (outreach sent, price approved, deal closed, sensitive data accessed) must be logged with: date, action, who approved it, and what was approved.

This log is:

- append-only in normal operation (no silent edits to past entries),
- reviewable by the CEO at any time,
- the source of truth if there is ever a dispute about what was approved.

The specific storage location/register for this log is to be confirmed by the Founder; until then, logs are kept in the Sales Report (Section 6) "Last Action" trail plus dated entries in project/operations records.

---

# 12. Client Success Loop

After delivery close-out:

1. Ask the client for feedback (what worked, what didn't).
2. Record whether the delivered outcome matched the proposal's promise.
3. Identify any follow-on opportunity (expansion, referral, renewal) — routed back through the Lead Qualification Model (Section 4) rather than assumed.
4. Flag any client-approved result as candidate content for YouTube/marketing (STRAT-001 Sections 11, 14), pending explicit consent.

---

# 13. Lessons Learned Loop

After each closed deal (won or lost) and each delivery close-out:

1. Record what was assumed (pricing, timeline, scope) versus what actually happened.
2. Record any point where an approval gate caught a problem — this validates the gate and should not be treated as friction to remove.
3. Record any point where the process felt too slow or too loose, and propose (not implement) an adjustment for CEO review.
4. Feed validated lessons into the Knowledge Register so future leads, pricing, and delivery decisions improve over time, consistent with `aos-learning-loop`.
