# 1. Purpose

This document sets the business model order and cash priority for ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP's first revenue push: which engine launches first, what must be built now, what must wait, cautious 30-day targets, expense caps, risk warnings, and the CEO approval gates that keep the launch safe, legal, and controlled.

---

# 2. Current Situation

AOS has built internal architecture, Partner governance, the Partner Factory, and Guardian's defensive review function (PRJ-001 through PRJ-006). None of that work has yet produced a paying client or revenue. The Founder now needs a practical, sequenced path from architecture into cash, without abandoning AOS discipline.

---

# 3. Revenue Priority Decision

ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP will not try to build five companies at once. The first revenue engine is **AOS AI Services**, because it can generate revenue faster than YouTube monetization, dropshipping, or software SaaS — a single service client can be found, sold, and paid within days using skills AOS already has documented. This is a sequencing decision, not a decision to abandon the other business lines; content, website, and ecommerce work continues in support roles from day one.

---

# 4. Business Model Order

| Order | Engine | Role |
|-------|--------|------|
| 1 | AOS AI Services | First cash engine — sold directly to small business clients |
| 2 | Multi-platform content | Supporting engine — builds lead generation and future audience monetization |
| 3 | Dropshipping / ecommerce | Later, controlled experiment — small budget, explicit kill/scale gates |
| 4 | AOS software / customer deployment | Later product — built only once services revenue is proven |

---

# 5. First Cash Engine: AOS AI Services

AI Services is prioritized because delivery uses documentation, drafting, dashboard, and workflow skills AOS already has proven internally (The Librarian, Atlas, Markdown Audit Tool, Partner Factory templates). The Service Offer Catalog (STRAT-007) turns these into priced, deliverable packages a small business can buy immediately.

---

# 6. Supporting Engine: Multi-Platform Content

Content across YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, Facebook, Threads, and LinkedIn is not the first month's cash source. Its job is lead generation: demonstrating AOS capability, building trust, and feeding the client pipeline (STRAT-008) and the Multi-Platform Content Factory (STRAT-010). Any monetization from views, sponsorships, or affiliate links is a later, secondary outcome.

---

# 7. Later Experiment: Dropshipping / Ecommerce

Dropshipping and marketplace selling (STRAT-011) is treated strictly as a controlled experiment, not a funding source for month one. It requires CEO product approval before any listing, a capped ad budget, and explicit test/pause/scale/kill decision gates. It runs in parallel at small scale, never at the expense of services delivery capacity.

---

# 8. Later Product: AOS Software / Customer Deployment

Packaging AOS itself (Atlas-style tooling, dashboards, Partner workflows) as a customer-facing software product is deferred until AI Services revenue is proven and repeatable. Building this earlier would repeat the trap PRJ-007 exists to break: more internal system-building before any client has paid.

---

# 9. What Must Be Built Now

- Service Offer Catalog with real prices (STRAT-007)
- Lead pipeline, qualification, and outreach rules (STRAT-008)
- Manual channel/account setup runbook (STRAT-009)
- Content factory workflow definitions (STRAT-010)
- Static website / landing page plan (STRAT-012)
- Revenue dashboard spec and templates (STRAT-013, template pack)
- 14-Day Revenue Sprint execution plan (STRAT-006)

---

# 10. What Must Wait

- Any real account creation (email, social, website hosting, marketplace) — CEO manual action only
- Any paid advertising or paid API usage
- Any dropshipping product listing
- Any new Partner activation (Marketing Partner, Reporting Partner, Client Acquisition Partner remain Proposed/future)
- A customer-facing AOS software product
- Scaling content production beyond what one Founder can sustainably review and approve

---

# 11. First 14 Days

The first 14 days build and test the revenue engine end to end: offers, website copy, lead research, outreach templates, proposal template, delivery workflow, content calendar, first content batch, first 25 prospects, a CEO-approved outreach batch, follow-up workflow, demo/dashboard prep, pricing review, and a first close attempt with lessons learned. See `AOS_14_Day_Revenue_Sprint.md` (STRAT-006) for the full day-by-day plan.

---

# 12. First 30 Days

Weeks 1–2 follow the 14-Day Sprint. Weeks 3–4 repeat and refine: a second outreach batch (CEO-approved), first client delivery if a deal closed, continued content posting, dashboard review, and a 30-day retrospective feeding lessons back into the Service Offer Catalog and outreach rules.

---

# 14. Expense Caps

No spend occurs without a specific CEO-approved amount and purpose. The AED 1,000–6,000 first-30-day cap (excluding legal setup) covers optional low-cost tools, a domain name if approved, and small experiment budgets — not open-ended spending. Any paid API usage requires a completed budget approval per CLAUDE.md Section 4 and stays out of scope for this launch unless separately approved.

---

# 15. Risk Warnings

- **Legal/compliance:** outreach, content, and ecommerce activity must follow applicable consumer protection, advertising, and data-privacy rules; Guardian reviews account and data-handling risk before real accounts are used.
- **Reputational:** unreviewed outreach or content can damage trust faster than it builds it; nothing sends or publishes without CEO approval.
- **Cost:** uncapped spend on ads, tools, or product testing can erode a thin early budget; every spend needs explicit approval.
- **Over-promising:** revenue targets in this plan and in client-facing materials must never be stated as guarantees.
- **Focus dilution:** attempting all five business lines simultaneously risks finishing none of them; the Business Model Order (Section 4) exists specifically to prevent this.

---

# 16. CEO Approval Gates

| Action | Approval Required Before |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Sending any outreach email or DM | Message content and recipient list approved |
| Publishing any content | Content and platform approved |
| Creating any ads | Ad content, budget, and platform approved |
| Creating any real account | Account name, purpose, and security setup approved |
| Spending any money | Specific amount and purpose approved |
| Agreeing pricing or closing a deal | Final price and terms approved |
| Signing any contract | Contract terms approved |
| Listing or advertising any product | Product, supplier, and compliance review approved |
| Activating a new Partner | Full Partner Factory lifecycle (PCL-001) complete |

---

# 17. Atlas Operating Model

Atlas coordinates the Revenue Launch package: tracks sprint and dashboard progress, drafts outreach/content/proposal materials, researches and scores leads, and prepares CEO briefings. Atlas never sends outreach, publishes content, spends money, agrees pricing, closes a deal, signs a contract, or creates a real account on its own authority — every one of those actions requires explicit CEO approval first.

# 19. Dashboards Required

A local revenue dashboard tracking leads, outreach, deals, revenue, expenses, and content performance is required before scaling outreach volume. See `AOS_Revenue_Dashboard_Spec.md` (STRAT-013) for the full KPI list, data model, and CEO briefing format.

# 20. Final CTO Recommendation

Sequence services first. Do not build five companies at once. Launch the Service Offer Catalog and lead pipeline immediately, use content and website work only to support lead generation, keep the dropshipping experiment small and gated, and defer a customer-facing AOS software product until services revenue is proven. Every outreach, publish, spend, pricing, and account-creation action stays behind an explicit CEO approval gate throughout.
