# 1. Purpose

This document defines how AOS identifies and prioritizes leads and markets for the AOS AI Workflow Starter Pack, now repositioned as "3 automations for the price of one" ($399 USD). It replaces any earlier geography-first framing for lead sourcing with a pain-point-first approach, while keeping AOS's revenue launch flexible enough to work anywhere a genuine fit exists.

---

# 2. Pain-Point-First Principle

AOS identifies businesses by **observable pain, problem, or opportunity signals first** — not by country. A business that visibly runs on memory instead of systems (lost leads, undocumented internal work, no visibility for the owner) is a stronger lead than a business in a "preferred" country with no visible pain at all.

This keeps the pipeline flexible instead of artificially narrowing it to one market before AOS has any real client data to justify a narrower focus.

---

# 3. Geography-Second Principle

Once a business shows a real pain-point match, geography only matters for **practical delivery reasons**, not eligibility:

| Practical Factor | Why It Matters | Why It's Not a Hard Gate |
|-------------------|-----------------|----------------------------|
| Payment | The approved payment link is a USD PayPal checkout | PayPal accepts payment from most countries; USD pricing works broadly |
| Language | Delivery is currently English-only | Many businesses worldwide operate comfortably in English |
| Timezone | Affects consultation-call scheduling, not eligibility | Async intake (forms, written discovery) reduces the impact |
| Legal/marketplace specifics | UAE/GCC and other region-specific rules matter for **ecommerce/marketplace** work | Does not apply to this services offer — see Section 6 |

---

# 4. Pain-Point Signal Categories

| Signal | What It Looks Like | Best-Fit Automation |
|--------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Manual lead handling | Leads mentioned as tracked "in my head," a notebook, or scattered messages | 1 — Lead / Client Tracking Automation |
| No documented internal process | Job posts or public content describing repetitive, undocumented internal tasks | 2 — Internal Operations Automation |
| No reporting/visibility | Owner describes finding out how the business is doing "too late" or relies on gut feel | 3 — CEO / Management Visibility Automation |
| Visibly overloaded founder/owner | Public complaints or content about being stretched too thin doing routine tasks | All three — strong general fit |
| Growing team, no SOPs | Hiring signals combined with no visible documentation/process content | 2 — Internal Operations Automation |

Atlas may use these signal categories to score and prioritize leads it is given (per STRAT-008's scoring model). Atlas does not search for or contact real businesses on its own — lead identification is manual or a separately CEO-approved research action.

---

# 5. Simple First-Market Framework

This is a **starting heuristic, not a restriction.** AOS starts looking where reach is easiest, then expands as real results create patterns worth following:

1. **Start with reachability.** Begin with the Founder's existing network and communities the Founder can genuinely reach (professional contacts, local business groups, relevant online communities) — not because they're the "right" market, but because warm reach produces faster, more honest signal than cold outreach into the unknown.
2. **Prioritize English-comfortable, USD/PayPal-friendly businesses.** This keeps delivery and payment simple while AOS is still a one-person operation.
3. **Do not exclude by country.** A qualified pain-point lead outside the Founder's home region is not turned away — it is simply handled with async-first communication (written intake, recorded loom-style walkthroughs if needed) rather than assuming a live call is required.
4. **Let real results guide focus.** Once AOS has delivered a handful of real engagements, revisit this framework and let the *actual* pattern of who converts and who succeeds — not a guess made before any data existed — narrow the focus if narrowing turns out to help.
