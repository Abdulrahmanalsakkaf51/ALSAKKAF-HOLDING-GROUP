# 1. Purpose

This document defines how AOS plans, produces, and reviews multi-platform content. It is a **workflow and planning document only** — it does not publish anything. No content leaves draft/queued status without explicit CEO review and approval, per PRJ-007's CEO Approval Gates. Publishing itself is out of scope for this document.

Content's near-term job is **lead generation for AOS AI Services**, not direct content monetization. Direct monetization (Section 11) is a later path, not the first-month goal.

---

# 2. YouTube Long-Form Workflow

1. Pick a topic from the Content Pillars (Section 9).
2. Draft a short outline: hook, problem, walkthrough, takeaway, call-to-action to the website/DM.
3. Record in one batch session (see Section 8).
4. Edit: trim dead air, add captions, add a simple intro/outro.
5. Write title, description, and tags using the templates in Section 10.
6. Queue as a draft/private upload — CEO reviews before it goes public.

---

# 3. YouTube Shorts Workflow

1. Pull a 30–60 second clip from an existing long-form recording, or record a single vertical take answering one question.
2. Add captions (most viewers watch muted).
3. Write a short hook-first title.
4. Queue as a draft — CEO reviews before it goes public.

---

# 4. Instagram Reels Workflow

1. Reuse the Shorts cut where the framing fits, or record a native vertical take.
2. Keep the first 1–2 seconds a visual hook.
3. Add on-screen text and captions.
4. Write caption using the template in Section 10.
5. Queue as a draft — CEO reviews before it goes public.

---

# 5. TikTok Workflow

1. Reuse the same vertical cut used for Reels/Shorts where appropriate.
2. Keep the tone slightly more casual/direct than LinkedIn.
3. Add captions and on-screen text.
4. Queue as a draft — CEO reviews before it goes public.

---

# 6. Facebook Reels/Page Workflow

1. Cross-post the same vertical cut used for Reels/TikTok.
2. For the Page feed, share the long-form video link with a short summary post.
3. Queue as a draft — CEO reviews before it goes public.

---

# 7. Threads Workflow

1. Turn the video's core insight into a short text post (2–5 sentences).
2. Link to the full video or website where relevant.
3. Queue as a draft — CEO reviews before it goes public.

---

# 8. LinkedIn Post Workflow

1. Write a longer-form text post (business tone) expanding on the same topic — the "why this matters for a small business" angle.
2. Reference the long-form video or a client-relevant insight (no real client data without approval).
3. Queue as a draft — CEO reviews before it goes public.

# 11. Original-Content Rule

**Content must be owned, licensed, public domain, or meaningfully transformed with original commentary or education.** Every piece of content published under the AOS brand must pass this test before it is queued.

---

# 12. Reused-Content Warning

Reposting someone else's video, image, or audio without rights, a license, or meaningful transformation risks copyright takedowns, platform strikes, demonetization, and account bans — and undermines the trust the brand is trying to build. **Do not build the company on stolen clips or low-effort reused content.**

---

# 13. Shorts Repurposing Legal Rule

- Repurposing **your own** long-form recording into Shorts/Reels/TikTok cuts is fine — it is your content.
- Repurposing **someone else's** content requires an explicit license, verified fair-use with real added commentary/analysis/education, or public-domain status.
- When rights are unclear: do not use the clip. Choose an original alternative instead.

# 14. Daily Content Workflow

```text
Idea -> Script/Outline -> Record/Design -> Edit -> Caption/Title/Hashtags -> CEO review (first-time or sensitive topics) -> Queue (not auto-published)
```

Routine, already-approved-format content may queue directly for the CEO's scheduled publish pass. Any new format, claim, client reference, or sensitive topic requires a specific CEO review before queuing.

---

# 15. 2–3 Videos/Day Workflow (Batching Model)

This does not mean filming live every day. It means:

1. **Weekly batch record**: one session records enough long-form + vertical raw footage for the week.
2. **Daily edit**: one clip edited per day from the batch.
3. **Daily queue**: one edited piece queued per platform per day, drawn from the batch, until the week's footage is used.

This produces a steady daily output (2–3 pieces/day across platforms) without requiring daily filming.

---

# 16. Analytics Review Loop

Weekly, review per platform: views, watch time/completion rate, follower change, and click-throughs to the website or DM. Decide: double down on the top-performing pillar/format, retire the lowest performer, and adjust the next week's batch topics accordingly. Record the review in the Revenue Dashboard (STRAT-013).

# 18. Revenue Paths

## Lead Generation (Primary, Near-Term)

Content's main job right now is driving small business owners to the website or a DM, where they can be routed into the Client Lead Pipeline (STRAT-008). This is the only revenue path content is expected to support in the first 30 days.

## Affiliate Links (Secondary, Near-Term Once Relevant)

Once content has a real audience, relevant tool/software affiliate links may be added to descriptions — only for tools AOS actually uses and can honestly recommend, and only with CEO approval.

## Sponsorship (Later)

Considered only once the channel has a genuine, engaged audience — not part of the first 30–90 days.

## Digital Products (Later)

Templates, guides, or mini-courses built from AOS's own service delivery experience — considered only after real client delivery work exists to draw from.

## Platform Monetization (Later)

YouTube Partner Program or equivalent — considered only once eligibility thresholds are naturally reached through consistent original content; not a near-term target.
