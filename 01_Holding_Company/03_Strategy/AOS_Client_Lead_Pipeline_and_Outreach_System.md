# 1. Purpose

This document defines how AOS finds, qualifies, scores, contacts, and converts leads into paying AI Services clients — safely, legally, and without spam — while keeping every irreversible action gated behind explicit CEO approval.

---

# 2. Atlas Authority Limits

Atlas may research, score, draft, summarize, and recommend.

Atlas may not send outreach, publish messages, accept deals, agree pricing, or sign contracts without CEO approval.

After the CEO approves a campaign rule and message template, Atlas may prepare batches and track progress, but sending still requires the approved method and compliance rules.

This limit applies to every lead, every channel, and every deal size — there is no exception for a "small" or "safe-looking" message.

---

# 3. Lead Source Categories

| Category | Description | Method |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Existing network | Founder's personal and professional contacts | Manual — Founder-initiated |
| Referrals | Introductions from existing clients or contacts | Manual — relationship-based |
| Public business directories | Publicly listed business information (e.g. chamber of commerce listings, public company pages) | Manual review of publicly available pages only — no scraping or scanning tools |
| LinkedIn (manual browsing) | Publicly visible profiles and company pages | Manual browsing and note-taking only — no automated scraping, no bulk connection requests |
| Local business associations / events | Business groups, networking events, industry meetups | Manual, in-person or direct contact |
| Inbound interest | Anyone who contacts AOS AI Services first (via website, social, referral) | Always highest priority — already opted in |

No lead source in this system involves scraping, scanning, automated data harvesting, or purchased contact lists.

---

# 4. Lead Qualification Criteria

A lead is qualified for outreach only if it meets all of the following:

1. It is a real, identifiable small business or professional with a plausible need for at least one catalog offer (STRAT-007).
2. There is a legitimate, traceable way to contact them (public business email, public contact form, LinkedIn, or a warm introduction) — not a personal/private contact obtained without consent.
3. There is no indication the business has asked not to be contacted (e.g. "no solicitation" notices).
4. The business appears active and operating (recent activity, live website/social presence, or a personal referral confirms it).

# 5. Lead Scoring Table

| Factor | 0 points | 1 point | 2 points |
|--------|----------|---------|----------|
| Fit (need for an AOS offer) | No visible need | Possible need | Clear, visible need (e.g. outdated/no website, messy operations, no content presence) |
| Budget signal | No signal / appears very small budget | Some signal (established business, staff, paid tools visible) | Strong signal (multiple locations, active hiring, visible marketing spend) |
| Urgency | No urgency indicators | Some indicators (recent complaint, visible gap, recent launch) | Clear urgency (explicit stated need, recent inbound interest) |
| Reachability | No safe/traceable contact method | Indirect contact method (general inquiry form) | Direct, appropriate contact method or warm introduction available |

**Priority tiers:**

| Total Score | Priority Tier |
|-------------|----------------|
| 0–2 | Low — deprioritize |
| 3–5 | Medium — include in standard outreach batch |
| 6–8 | High — prioritize for first outreach batches and personalized messaging |

# 6. Outreach Rules

- Channels: email, LinkedIn message, or a platform-appropriate DM only — never SMS/WhatsApp cold contact without an existing relationship.
- Tone: professional, specific to the recipient's business, never generic mass-blast language.
- Never claim: guaranteed results, fake urgency, false scarcity, false social proof, or any client/revenue figure that has not actually happened.
- Frequency cap: no more than 1 initial message + up to 2 follow-ups per lead within a 14-day window (see Follow-Up Schedule, Section 12). No repeated contact after an explicit "not interested" or opt-out response.
- Every outreach message must offer a clear, easy way to decline further contact.

---

# 7. Compliance Rules

- No impersonation of another person, business, or authority.
- No misleading subject lines or message content.
- No purchased or scraped contact lists.
- Respect any stated "do not contact" or unsubscribe/opt-out request immediately and permanently for that contact.
- General caution note: this document does not constitute legal advice. Before any formal contract, terms of service, or large-scale campaign, the CEO should obtain a proper legal review appropriate to the relevant UAE/GCC and any other applicable jurisdiction rules.

---

# 8. Email Safety Rules

- Use a dedicated business email address for outreach, never a personal inbox, per the naming plan in `AOS_Channel_and_Account_Setup_Runbook.md` (STRAT-009).
- No attachments in a first cold email (reduces spam-filter risk and recipient suspicion).
- Keep first-contact emails short (under ~150 words) with one clear next step.
- Never request payment, credentials, or sensitive data by email.

---

# 9. Social DM Safety Rules

- Only message accounts/pages that are clearly business accounts, not personal profiles, unless a business owner is directly and identifiably reachable that way.
- No mass/bulk automated DM sending — every DM is prepared individually or from an approved template and sent manually or through an approved, CEO-sign-off method.
- No follower-buying, engagement-pod, or automation-tool shortcuts of any kind.

---

# 10. No-Spam Principle

```text
If a message would be sent to someone who has not shown any real, traceable connection to AOS AI Services or a genuine, specific reason to be contacted, it is not sent.

Quality and relevance over volume, always.
```

# 11. CEO Approval Gates

| Gate | What Requires Approval |
|------|--------------------------|
| Campaign rule approval | The overall outreach approach, channel, and targeting logic for a batch |
| Message template approval | The exact wording of every outreach email/DM template before it is used |
| Sending approval | The specific recipient list and go-ahead to send a prepared batch |
| Pricing approval | The final price quoted to any specific client |
| Proposal approval | The full content of any proposal before it is sent |
| Deal close approval | The final agreed terms before a deal is considered closed |

No outreach is sent, no price is quoted, and no deal is closed without the CEO clearing the relevant gate above.

# 13. Pricing Approval Rule

Atlas may recommend a price from the ranges defined in `AOS_Service_Offer_Catalog.md` (STRAT-007) based on client scope and complexity. Atlas never finalizes, quotes, or confirms a price to a client. The CEO gives explicit final sign-off on price before it is included in any proposal or communicated to the client.

---

# 14. Proposal Approval Rule

Every proposal, regardless of price or client size, is reviewed and explicitly approved by the CEO before it is sent. Atlas prepares the proposal draft using `Proposal_Template.md` (RLT-005); Atlas does not send it independently.

---

# 15. Deal Close Rule

The CEO agrees all final terms (price, scope, timeline, payment terms) directly with the client. Atlas documents the close in the Lead Tracker and Sales Report once the CEO confirms it — Atlas does not negotiate, counter-offer, or accept terms on AOS's behalf at any point.

---

# 16. Client Delivery Handoff

Once the CEO confirms a deal is closed:

1. Atlas moves the lead's status to "Won" in the Lead Tracker (RLT-001) and records final agreed scope and price.
2. Atlas prepares the relevant `Service_Delivery_Checklist.md` (RLT-006) for the specific offer sold.
3. Delivery work begins only after the CEO confirms the engagement is ready to start (e.g. any deposit or agreement step the CEO requires is complete).
4. Atlas tracks delivery milestones and prepares the `Client_Report_Template.md` (RLT-007) at agreed checkpoints.

---

# 17. Audit Log Requirements

Every lead and outreach action is logged with: date, action taken, who took it (Atlas-prepared vs. CEO-approved/executed), and outcome. This log lives in the Lead Tracker (RLT-001) and the Sales Report (Section 12). Guardian may review this log for data-sensitivity or process-risk concerns consistent with its Cybersecurity Risk Review Skill (GSKILL-001); Guardian does not access real client payment or credential data, which is never stored in this log.

---

# 18. Follow-Up Schedule

| Day | Action |
|-----|--------|
| Day 0 | Initial outreach sent (after CEO approval) |
| Day 3 | First follow-up if no reply (after CEO approval of follow-up content) |
| Day 7 | Second follow-up if still no reply (after CEO approval) |
| Day 14 | Close out the lead as "No Response" if still no reply; no further contact without a new, specific reason |

A reply at any point pauses this schedule and moves the lead into an active conversation, handled directly with CEO involvement per the approval gates above.
