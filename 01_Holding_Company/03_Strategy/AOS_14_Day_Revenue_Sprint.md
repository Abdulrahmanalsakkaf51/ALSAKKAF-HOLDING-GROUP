# 1. Purpose

This document is the day-by-day execution plan for the first 14 days of the AOS Revenue Launch. It converts the AOS Revenue Launch Master Plan (STRAT-005) into daily objectives, deliverables, responsible Partners, tools, CEO approval checkpoints, expected cost, and dashboard updates.

---

# 2. Sprint Ground Rules

- Days 1–13 are entirely preparation and drafting: no outreach is sent, no content is published, no ad is created, no real account is created, and no money is spent on any of those days.
- Day 9 "research" means manual or documentation-based prospect research only — reading public business listings, websites, and directories by hand. No scraping, scanning, or automated data-collection tooling is used; any tool that would require network access beyond normal browsing needs separate CEO approval first.
- Day 10's "prepare outreach batch" means Atlas drafts and organizes the batch for review. It does not send anything. Sending requires a separate, explicit CEO approval of the approved outreach method and the compliance rules defined in `AOS_Client_Lead_Pipeline_and_Outreach_System.md` (STRAT-008).
- Expected cost is AED 0 on every day unless a specific line item is flagged for CEO approval.

# 4. Sprint Completion Criteria

The 14-Day Revenue Sprint is complete when:

- All templates and workflows listed above exist and have been reviewed by the CEO
- 25 candidate leads have been identified and qualified
- At least one outreach batch has been prepared for CEO approval (sending itself may or may not have occurred yet)
- A first client close attempt has been made and its outcome, win or not, is documented
- Lessons learned are recorded in the PRJ-007 progress log for the next sprint cycle

Reaching Day 14 does not require a closed deal to count as complete — it requires the full loop (research → qualify → draft → CEO approval → attempt → learn) to have run at least once under AOS discipline.
