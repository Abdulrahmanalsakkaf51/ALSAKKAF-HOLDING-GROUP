# 1. Call Structure (20-30 minutes)

| Phase | Minutes | Goal |
|-------|---------|------|
| Open | 2 | Set agenda, put them at ease |
| Discover | 10-12 | Understand their week, pains, and current AI usage |
| Match | 5-7 | Map their pains to the three agents — or say honestly if it's not a fit |
| Close | 3-5 | Agree on a concrete next step |

---

# 2. Open

> "Thanks for making time. Here's how I'd suggest we use the next 20 minutes: I ask about how your business runs day to day, then I explain what I'd build for you and you decide if it's interesting. If it's not a fit, I'll say so plainly — deal?"

# 3. Discover (listen more than talk)

1. "Walk me through a normal working day — where does your time actually go?"
2. "What's the work that keeps landing back on your desk because nobody else knows how to do it?"
3. "When you need to know something — about a competitor, a customer, a market change — how do you find out today?"
4. "Are you using any AI tools now? What worked, what didn't?"
5. "If one part of your week ran itself with your approval, which part would you pick?"

Take notes in their words — they become the proposal's personalization.

# 4. Match

> "Based on what you said, here's what the three agents would look like for you..."

- Manager Agent → tie to their answer on #1 and #5
- Research / Analyst Agent → tie to their answer on #3
- Operations / Implementation Agent → tie to their answer on #2

Honesty checkpoint: if their real need is something else (a website, custom software, integrations), say so and offer a custom quote instead — do not force the pack.

# 5. Close

> "The whole package — three custom agents, the routing map, workflow, guide, safety rules, and a 30-minute setup session — is $450 one-time, delivered in 5-7 business days from your intake answers. I'll send a written proposal today so you can see everything in black and white. If it reads right, you reply 'let's go' and we start. Fair?"

Then: confirm their email, send the proposal (AIAT-001) after CEO review, and log the call in the sales tracker.

# 6. What Never to Say

- Any guarantee of revenue, growth, or results
- "Fully autonomous" / "AI employee" / "runs your business for you"
- Any claim about other clients that isn't literally true
