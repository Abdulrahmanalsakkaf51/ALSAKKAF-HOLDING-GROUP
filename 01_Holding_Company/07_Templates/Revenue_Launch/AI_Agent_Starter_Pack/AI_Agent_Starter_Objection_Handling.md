# 1. Ground Rules

Never argue, never overpromise, never invent proof. If the honest answer loses the sale, the honest answer was still correct — a client won on a false promise becomes a refund and a reputation problem.

---

# 2. Objections and Honest Responses

**"I can just use ChatGPT for free."**
> "You can — and the agents run inside a tool like that. What you're paying for is the design: prompts built around your business, a routing system so you know what to ask when, safety rules, and a working setup in a week instead of months of trial and error. If you enjoy building that yourself, you genuinely don't need me."

**"$450 is a lot for some prompts."**
> "If it were three generic prompts, it would be. It's an intake on your business, three tested agent designs, a routing map, a documented workflow, a reporting template, safety rules, and a live session — the prompts are the smallest part. And it's one-time, not monthly."

**"How do I know it works? Do you have case studies?"**
> "We're a new service, and I won't invent social proof. What I can show you is exactly what you'd receive — the real document structure — before you pay. We also run our own company on this exact discipline. And there's a revision round included, so what's delivered gets adjusted to fit."

**"Will this replace my staff / run on its own?"**
> "No, and be careful with anyone who says yes. These are assistants: they draft, research, and organize — you and your team stay the decision-makers. That's a safety feature, not a limitation."

**"I don't have time to learn a new system."**
> "The guide is written for a busy owner: setup is about 15 minutes, and the 30-minute session gets you running your first real tasks with me on the call. If you have time to explain a task to a person, you have time to use these."

**"Can you connect it to my email/CRM/WhatsApp?"**
> "Not in this pack — integrations are custom work I'd scope and quote separately. The Starter Pack deliberately needs no technical setup, which is why it's a week and $450 instead of months and thousands."

**"Not now / no budget."**
> "Completely fair. Want me to leave you the one-page overview so it's on file when the timing's right?" — then stop selling. Log the lead as future follow-up.

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**
> "The price is fixed because the scope is fixed. What I can do is point you to the $399 Workflow Starter Pack if systems-documentation is the more urgent need — it's a different deliverable, not a discount."

---

# 3. When to Walk Away

- They want guaranteed results or revenue commitments
- They want the agents to send messages/act on accounts autonomously
- They want trading/investment automation
- The real need is custom software they don't want to pay custom prices for

Politely decline or redirect to a custom quote. Log the conversation honestly.
