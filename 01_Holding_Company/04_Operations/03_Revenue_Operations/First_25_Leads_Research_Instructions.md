# 1. Purpose

Step-by-step manual instructions for finding and logging the first 25 leads for AOS AI Services, consistent with `Lead_Research_Operating_Model.md` (RLOP-001). This is manual, human research — no scraping, no automated tools, no network access by Claude or Atlas without separate approval.

---

# 2. Where to Look

| Source | How |
|--------|-----|
| Personal network | List 5-10 people you already know who run or manage a small business |
| Public business directories | Browse a local chamber of commerce or SME association directory by hand |
| LinkedIn (manual) | Search small-business owner/manager job titles in your target city; read public profiles yourself |
| Local business associations / coworking spaces | Note businesses you know are members, from public listings |

---

# 3. What Qualifies as a Fit

- Small business or solo professional (not a large enterprise)
- Visible sign of manual, repetitive workflow pain (e.g. no automation, messy spreadsheets, no dashboard, inconsistent content)
- A reachable decision maker (owner, manager, or someone with authority to say yes)
- A real, findable public presence (website, LinkedIn page, or business listing)

---

# 4. How to Score Each Lead

Use the scoring model in `AOS_Client_Lead_Pipeline_and_Outreach_System.md` (STRAT-008): score fit, budget signal, urgency, and reachability, then assign the resulting priority tier. Only log a lead as "Qualified" once it has been scored.

---

# 5. How to Log a Lead

1. Open `Lead_Tracker.csv`.
2. Add one row per lead, filling every column you can verify (leave unknowns blank rather than guessing).
3. Set Status to "New" until qualified, then "Qualified" once scored.
4. Set "CEO Approval Needed" to Yes for every lead before any outreach is drafted or sent.
5. Never invent a company name, contact, or detail — only log what is genuinely, publicly verifiable.

---

# 6. Target

25 logged, qualified leads before Day 10 of the 14-Day Revenue Sprint (`AOS_14_Day_Revenue_Sprint.md`, STRAT-006), so the first outreach batch has a real pool to draw from.
