# 1. Purpose

This package answers, in plain language, every question an honest small-business client should ask before paying. It is written to be used directly: in emails, on calls, on the website, and in the handover session. Every statement here must stay consistent with CDP-001; if the two ever disagree, CDP-001 wins and this document gets fixed.

# 2. How the 3-Partner Starter Works

You buy the AI Agent Starter Pack ($450 USD, one scoped engagement). Here is the whole process in plain steps:

1. You pay through the approved payment link and answer an intake questionnaire about your business and the workflow you want help with.
2. Together we map that workflow — what comes in, what should go out, who decides.
3. We build three AI Partner roles fitted to that workflow. A typical set: a Manager (routes tasks, keeps the list), a Research/Analyst (reads and summarizes), an Operations Partner (produces documents).
4. We define the rules: what each Partner may read, what it may draft, and the fixed rule that humans approve everything.
5. We test the roles against your real workflow with test tasks, and you review the outputs.
6. You accept (one revision round is included), we deliver everything, and walk you through it in a 30-minute session.

Timeline: 5-7 business days from complete intake answers.

# 5. The Honest Part — AI Account Dependency

Read this before buying; we would rather lose a sale than surprise you:

1. The Partner roles need an AI service to run on. In client-owned mode, that means you need your own AI subscription (typically $20-30/month per seat, paid to the AI provider, not to us).
2. If your AI provider changes its models or pricing, behavior can shift. The role documents are written to be provider-portable, and we tell you which service we tested them on.
3. AI usage costs are yours in client-owned mode. For a typical small-business workflow this is small, but it is not zero and we will not pretend otherwise.
4. AI makes mistakes. That is exactly why every Partner drafts and a human decides. Any vendor promising you unsupervised AI for business decisions is selling you risk.

---

# 6. Managed Mode vs Client-Owned Mode — Which Should You Pick

Client-owned (available now) is right if: you already pay for an AI subscription, you want no ongoing fees to us, and you are comfortable pasting a role prompt and working in a chat interface. You own everything and depend on us for nothing after delivery.

Managed mode (planned, not yet offered) will be right if: you want outputs without operating anything yourself, and you prefer a monthly service relationship. Until we offer it officially, we will not take managed-mode money — if someone on our side offers you managed mode today, ask for the Founder.

There is no lock-in in either mode: the role documents, workflows, and templates are yours in readable files either way.

# 7. Security and Data Protection, Simply

1. We never ask for, collect, or store your passwords or account credentials. Never. Anyone claiming to be us and asking for credentials is not us.
2. Before any Partner reads any of your documents, we classify them together: what the AI may read, what it may never see. Regulated or highly sensitive data is excluded from the starter engagement entirely — that requires a custom, explicitly-scoped project.
3. In client-owned mode, your data goes only to your own AI provider under your own account and their terms — it never passes through us.
4. AI Partners get drafting authority only. Nothing is sent, paid, deleted, or published by AI in any deployment we deliver.
5. We put all of this in writing in your delivery package, so your rules survive staff changes.

---

# 8. Questions We Want You to Ask

1. "Can I see it working before I decide how to use it?" — Yes: the public Playground demos plus a live demonstration on a call (see DEMO-001).
2. "What happens if it writes something wrong?" — A human catches it at the approval step; that step is mandatory in our design.
3. "What if I cancel my AI subscription?" — The Partners stop running, but you keep every document; resubscribe or switch providers and continue.
4. "Do you see my business data?" — Only what you show us during setup, under the intake's confidentiality terms; in daily operation (client-owned mode), no.
