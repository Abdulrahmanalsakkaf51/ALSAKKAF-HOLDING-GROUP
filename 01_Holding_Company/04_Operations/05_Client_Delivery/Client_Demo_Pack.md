# 1. Purpose

These are the demonstrations the Founder uses in sales conversations, on the website, and in videos. Every example is written so a non-technical business owner understands it in under two minutes. All client names and data below are fictional and labeled as demonstrations, following the PAW-005 pattern. Capability claims stay within what AOS testing has demonstrated.

---

# 2. How It Works — The Two-Minute Explanation

Say it exactly like this:

"You get three AI Partners. A Partner is not a chatbot — it's an AI with a written job description: a role, rules, the documents it's allowed to read, and a hard rule that it drafts and you decide.

A Manager Partner routes work and keeps a task list. A Research Partner reads and summarizes. An Operations Partner produces the actual documents — trackers, reports, letters. They work inside YOUR workflow, which we map together first.

Nothing gets sent, published, or decided by AI. Every output lands in a drafts folder waiting for a human. That's not a limitation — that's the design."

---

# 3. Demo 1 — Weekly Reporting Workflow (real, reproducible)

**The client's problem:** the owner spends 2-3 hours every Friday assembling a status report from scattered notes and spreadsheets.

**The deployed workflow:**

| Step | Who | What happens |
|------|-----|--------------|
| 1 | Client staff | Keep one simple tracker file updated during the week (we provide the template) |
| 2 | Operations Partner | Every Friday, turns the tracker into a drafted report: numbers counted (never estimated), overdue items flagged, one-page summary on top |
| 3 | Manager Partner | Checks the draft against the report rules (missing data marked "not recorded", zeros stay zeros) |
| 4 | Owner | Reads a finished draft, edits two lines, sends it. Ten minutes, not three hours |

**Why clients believe it:** we run our own company reports exactly this way, and we show ours (with our real internal tracker structure) in the demo.

---

# 4. Demo 2 — Client Intake to Proposal Workflow (fictional client, labeled)

**Scenario (fictional):** "Al Noor Trading", a 12-person supplies company, receives inquiries by email and loses track of them.

**The deployed workflow:**

1. A new inquiry arrives. Staff paste it into the intake form (one page, we provide it).
2. The Research Partner drafts a one-paragraph summary: what the prospect wants, size, urgency, missing information.
3. The Manager Partner adds it to the pipeline tracker with a follow-up date.
4. The Operations Partner drafts a tailored reply and a first proposal outline from the company's approved price list document.
5. The owner reviews the draft reply — nothing is ever sent automatically — edits, and sends.

**What changed:** no inquiry is forgotten, first response drops from days to hours, and the owner still writes nothing from scratch.

# 5. Demo 3 — Office Documents Workflow (real, reproducible)

**The client's problem:** recurring documents (trackers, letters, simple reports) each take 30-60 minutes of formatting work.

**The deployed workflow:** the Operations Partner produces real files — Excel trackers with correct columns and formulas-free honest counts, and Word letters from the company's own templates — using our Office Toolbelt approach (demonstrated in PRJ-013 with six passed task tests). Staff request a document in one sentence; a finished draft file appears in the drafts folder for human review.

**Live demo available:** this is the demo we can run on a call, because it uses our own tested tooling and fictional data.

# 6. Partner Deployment Example — What Delivery Actually Looks Like

For the fictional "Al Noor Trading" above, the delivered package would be:

1. Three Partner role documents (Manager, Research, Operations) written for their workflow.
2. A task routing map: which request goes to which Partner, what escalates to the owner.
3. The workflow documents and templates (intake form, pipeline tracker, report template).
4. Approval and safety rules on paper: AI drafts, humans decide; no credentials; no sensitive data without classification.
5. A 30-minute handover session.

Delivered in 5-7 business days after intake answers, per the AOS Three-Partner Customer Deployment Process (CDP-001). Deployment mode options (client's own AI account vs managed vs private) are explained in the Customer Education Package (EDU-001).
