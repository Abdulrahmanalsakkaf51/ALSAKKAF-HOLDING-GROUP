# 1. Purpose and Labeling Rule

This is an **original fictional series** created for the Sand Dunes Stories channel, distinct from the channel's real-history and real-folklore pillars (MEDIA-004 Section 4). It must never be presented as real folklore, real history, or a retelling of an existing traditional tale — it is new, owned, original fiction inspired by the desert setting.

**Mandatory on-screen/description labeling for every piece of content in this series:** "Original Fiction — Sand Dunes Stories" (or equivalent wording) must appear in the video description and, for the long-form episode, in an opening on-screen card within the first 15 seconds. This protects the channel's Section 5 Sourcing Rule differentiator (honest about what is claimed as real vs. invented) and must not be skipped, shortened, or omitted for any individual video without Founder approval.

# 2. Premise (one paragraph)

In a desert kingdom built around a single towering dune known as Telal — believed by its people to be alive, ancient, and watching — a young girl named Zara discovers she can hear what the dune has always been trying to say, right as an old, uneasy prophecy about Telal's fall begins to come true.

# 3. Core Characters

## 3.1 Zara

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Role | Protagonist |
| Age (approx.) | Early-to-mid teens — old enough to act alone, young enough that the adults around her still doubt her |
| Personality | Observant, stubborn, more curious than cautious; carries doubt about her own visions even as she trusts them |
| Visual motif | A pale blue-grey shawl/scarf inherited from her grandmother — the one recurring color anchor across all art for her character |

| Core flaw | Acts alone rather than asking for help, which repeatedly makes things harder than they needed to be |

## 3.2 Telal

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Role | The dune itself — not a literal talking character, but a near-sentient presence in the world, felt rather than heard by anyone except (partially) Zara |
| Nature | Ancient, slow, patient — communicates through tremors, shifting sand patterns, wind timing, and dreams, never in plain speech |
| Visual motif | A single dune, taller and darker-golden than the surrounding dunes, with a silhouette that subtly changes shape depending on its "mood" in a scene — always original art, never a real photographed location |
| Thematic role | Represents memory, warning, and consequence — the title "The Night Telal Fell" refers to a literal geological/supernatural event in-story, not a metaphor revealed until Episode 1 |

# 4. World Rules (continuity constraints — do not contradict in any script)

1. Telal is one specific dune, not a general term for "any dune." Other dunes in the world are ordinary.
2. Zara's ability is new — it did not exist before this story starts. She is not a hereditary "chosen one"; the prophecy is about Telal's fall, not about her specifically, and her connection to it is presented as circumstantial until/unless a future episode says otherwise.
3. No named character dies on-screen without a Founder-approved script revision — this is a young-audience-adjacent channel; consequence can be shown through loss of place, trust, or time, not graphic harm.
4. Magic/supernatural elements are limited to Telal's dune-based phenomena (tremors, sand shapes, wind, dreams). No unrelated magic system, no other supernatural characters, unless this bible is revised first.
5. Setting is a fictional desert kingdom, not a named real country, city, or historical period — avoid implying this is "ancient Arabia" as fact; it is inspired-by, explicitly fictional.

---

# 5. The Prophecy (source text — quote exactly when referenced in scripts)

"When the great dune forgets its own name, sand will fall like rain, and the one who first hears its silence will be the one who must answer it."

This line is the anchor for "The Night Telal Fell" and Episode 1. Any script referencing "the prophecy" should quote this exact wording rather than paraphrasing differently each time.
