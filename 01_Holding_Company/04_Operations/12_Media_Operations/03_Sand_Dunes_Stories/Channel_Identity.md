# 2. Channel Description (search field)

Stories from the Arabian sands — told properly. Lost cities, incense roads, pearl divers, falconers, poets, and the people who crossed the world's largest sand desert without a map. Real history, honest sources, and folklore clearly told as folklore. New stories regularly.

---

# 3. About Section Copy

The Arabian desert holds four thousand years of stories — trade empires, lost cities, survival knowledge, poetry wars, and legends told around fires.

Sand Dunes Stories tells them the way they deserve: carefully researched, honestly sourced, and narrated like stories, not lectures.

What you'll find here:

- Real history of Arabia and the Gulf — checked against real sources
- Desert knowledge: navigation, survival, falconry, and the ways of the Bedouin
- Folklore and legends — always labeled as folklore, never sold as fact
- The trade routes, cities, and seas that connected Arabia to the world

If you love history channels and want stories from a region usually reduced to a stereotype, you're in the right place.

---

# 4. Content Pillars

1. Lost places and trade routes (history)
2. Desert knowledge and survival (culture)
3. People of the desert: divers, falconers, poets, navigators (portraits)
4. Folklore and legends (clearly labeled)

---

# 5. Sourcing Rule

Every historical claim in a script carries a source note in the archive. Where historians disagree or evidence is thin (for example, the identification of Ubar), the script says so on screen. This honesty is the channel's differentiator, not a limitation.
