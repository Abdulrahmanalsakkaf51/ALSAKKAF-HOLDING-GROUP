# 0. Correction Notice (v2.0)

Version 1.0 of this bible invented an incorrect universe ("Telal and Zara" as a title, Telal as a sentient dune, Zara as protagonist, an invented brother "Naji" and an unnamed Seer, and an invented prophecy line). The Founder has supplied the actual canon below. **This version replaces that invented content.** The series title is **"Warrior of the Sand Dunes."** Zara and Telal are important parts of this universe, not the title or the central relationship — Telal is the capital city, not a character.

---

# 1. Purpose and Labeling Rule

This is an **original fictional series** created for the Sand Dunes Stories channel, distinct from the channel's real-history and real-folklore pillars (MEDIA-004 Section 4). It must never be presented as real folklore, real history, or a retelling of an existing traditional tale — it is new, owned, original fiction inspired by the desert setting.

**Mandatory on-screen/description labeling for every piece of content in this series:** "Original Fiction — Sand Dunes Stories: Warrior of the Sand Dunes" (or equivalent wording) must appear in the video description and, for the long-form episode, in an opening on-screen card within the first 15 seconds. This protects the channel's Section 5 Sourcing Rule differentiator (honest about what is claimed as real vs. invented) and must not be skipped, shortened, or omitted for any individual video without Founder approval.

# 2. Premise (one paragraph)

Telal is the capital of a desert kingdom, ruled by a King betrayed and overthrown in a single night by his own trusted commander, Sultan Feroos. As the city falls, the King's wife Leila escapes into the desert with her two sons — Amir, the elder, and Malik, the younger — hiding their true lineage from the world. Leila carries one more secret: a vial meant only for the son who is truly worthy, to be used only in the moment he stands between life and death. Years later, a seer named Zara looks into the eyes of a boy in a servant's cloak and sees what no one else has seen: the young Sultan King, hidden in plain sight.

# 5. World Rules (continuity constraints — do not contradict in any script)

1. Telal is the capital city of the kingdom — a place, not a person and not a supernatural entity.
2. Malik is the hidden heir despite being the younger brother; this is presented as the prophecy/Zara's recognition, not as a birthright by age. Do not reverse this.
3. On-screen violence stays non-graphic — the fall of Telal and Feroos's betrayal are shown through fire, flight, and consequence, not graphic harm to named characters. This is a young-audience-adjacent channel.
4. The potion's nature is not defined beyond "meant for the worthy son, to be used only in a life-or-death moment" — do not invent what it does mechanically until the Founder specifies; scripts should treat this as a mystery Malik himself doesn't understand yet.
5. Setting is a fictional desert kingdom inspired by the region, not a named real country, city, or historical period.
6. Do not invent additional canon facts (character names, the King's name, Jaber's specific actions, Amir/Malik's relationship dynamic) beyond what Section 3 establishes — flag as open items for the Founder instead, as done in Section 4.
