# Purpose

State plainly, in one short list, the categories of content that always require a Founder-authored response — never an Atlas-drafted one, and never anything sent without explicit Founder review.

---

# Scope

Applies to every reply, follow-up, and proposal produced anywhere under this system, regardless of channel.

---

# The List

1. **Contracts** — any agreement, terms of service, statement of work, or binding commitment.
2. **Financial commitments** — any promise of price, payment terms, timeline guarantee, or scope commitment beyond the two fixed-scope packages already published.
3. **Discounts** — any reduction from the published $399 / $450 prices, including the limited Founding Client Pilot pricing, which is applied for by email only and never auto-offered.
4. **Refunds** — any refund confirmation, amount, or process step.
5. **Legal opinions** — any statement about liability, intellectual property, compliance status, or dispute resolution.
6. **Data-security guarantees** — any claim about encryption, compliance certification, breach history, or data-safety promises not already documented and Founder-verified.
7. **Deadlines not verified** — any delivery date, response time, or completion promise that has not been explicitly confirmed by the Founder for that specific engagement.
8. **Customer complaints** — any substantive response to a complaint beyond acknowledging it and saying the Founder will follow up personally.

---

# The Rule

These categories always require a **Founder-authored** response — not an Atlas draft that the Founder edits lightly, but a response the Founder personally writes or personally and substantively approves word-for-word. Atlas may acknowledge that the message was received and state that the Founder will follow up (see the Escalation Matrix, COMMS-007, for the exact safe holding language per category) — Atlas must never attempt to answer the substantive question in any of these eight categories.

---

# How to Use

1. When classifying an inbound message (Communications Inbox Register, COMMS-002), check it against this list. If it matches any category, mark it accordingly and route it straight to the Founder.
2. The Reply Draft Template (COMMS-003) may only produce a holding acknowledgement for these categories — never a substantive answer.
3. This list is deliberately short and explicit so there is no ambiguity. If a new message does not clearly fit any of these eight categories but still feels sensitive, escalate anyway — when in doubt, this list errs toward more Founder involvement, not less.
