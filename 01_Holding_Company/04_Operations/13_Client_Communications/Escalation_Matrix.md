# Purpose

Map every sensitive category of inbound message to who must decide it and exactly what a holding reply may safely say while the Founder decides.

---

# Scope

This is a solo operation. For every category below, "who decides" is the Founder — Abdulrahman Alsakkaf — always. There is no delegate decision-maker for any category in this matrix. This document exists to make that explicit and to give Atlas a safe, honest holding response for each category instead of silence or a guess.

---

# Matrix

| Category | Why It Escalates | Who Decides | Safe Holding Reply (while waiting) | Must Never Say |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pricing (custom scope, discounts, negotiated terms) | Only the Founder sets or changes price; the two listed packages ($399 / $450) are fixed-scope, and anything outside them is "Request Custom Quote" only | Founder, always | "Thanks for asking — our standard packages are $399 (AI Workflow Starter Pack) and $450 (AI Agent Starter Pack). For anything outside those, let me get back to you with specifics." | Any discount, custom price, or "I can do X for you" commitment |
| Legal terms (contracts, liability, IP, terms of service disputes) | Legal commitments bind the company and carry real risk | Founder, always | "Good question — let me get back to you with specifics on that." | Any legal opinion, contract wording, liability acceptance, or IP assignment |
| Security / data (data handling, storage, breach questions, compliance claims) | Security claims must be accurate and are a Guardian (PARTNER-016) concern; false assurance is a real risk | Founder, always (Guardian may be consulted for review, but does not decide externally) | "Thanks for asking — data handling depends on your setup and the account you use. Let me get back to you with specifics rather than a general answer." | Any guarantee of security, compliance certification, or data-safety claim not already documented and verified |
| Complaints (dissatisfaction, service issues, refund-adjacent frustration) | Complaints need a considered, accountable response, not an automated one | Founder, always | "I'm sorry to hear that — I want to look into this properly and get back to you directly." | Any apology that admits fault before the facts are reviewed, any promise of compensation, or dismissing the complaint |
| Refund / payment (refund requests, payment disputes, chargeback threats) | Money movement is always a Founder decision; no automated system authorizes refunds | Founder, always | "Thanks for letting me know — I'll look into the payment/refund question and get back to you directly." | Any refund confirmation, refund amount, or payment promise |
| Custom scope (work outside the two fixed packages, "can you also do X") | Only the Founder can commit new scope or timeline | Founder, always | "That sounds like it could be a custom project — let me get back to you with specifics on scope and timing." | Any scope commitment, deadline, or deliverable list not already Founder-approved |

---

# How to Use

1. If an inbound message's classified intent (Communications Inbox Register, COMMS-002) matches any row above, the Reply Draft (COMMS-003) may only use the "Safe Holding Reply" language for that row — it must not attempt to answer the underlying question.
2. The item still goes to the Founder Approval Queue (COMMS-004) even though it is a holding reply — the holding reply itself still needs Founder sign-off before it is sent.
3. The Founder's actual substantive answer to any of these categories is Founder-authored, not Atlas-drafted — see `Forbidden_Auto_Send_Categories.md` (COMMS-009).
4. This matrix may be extended with new categories over time, but the "Who Decides" column never changes while this remains a solo operation.
