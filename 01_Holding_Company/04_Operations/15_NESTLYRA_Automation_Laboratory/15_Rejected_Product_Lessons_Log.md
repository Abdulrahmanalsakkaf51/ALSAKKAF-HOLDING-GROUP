# 2. Rules

1. Every rejection gets a row within one day of the decision, while the reasoning is fresh.
2. Failure Reason states facts (which criterion failed, by how much); Lesson Learned states what to do differently earlier or cheaper next time.
3. A pattern across three or more rejections triggers a review of the gate or the sourcing approach — logged as a Gate Change Proposed entry and queued for Founder decision (NAL-014).
4. Rows are permanent. A rejected product may re-enter the pipeline later only as a new candidate with fresh evidence, and its row here must be referenced in the new candidate's register entry.
5. This log follows the AOS learning-loop principle: rejection without a recorded lesson is waste; rejection with one is research.
