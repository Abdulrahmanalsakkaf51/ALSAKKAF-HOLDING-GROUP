# 1. Purpose

PRJ-007 exists to convert AOS from internal architecture into an immediate revenue engine using AI services, client acquisition, multi-platform content, lightweight websites, dashboards, and controlled ecommerce experiments.

This project produces the AOS Revenue Launch Operating Package: the master plan, the 14-day sprint, the service catalog, the lead and outreach system, the channel setup runbook, the content factory workflow, the dropshipping experiment plan, the website plan, the dashboard spec, a ready-to-use template pack, and a CEO briefing.

---

# 2. Strategic Question

```text
AOS has built architecture, Partners, a Partner Factory, and a Guardian review function. How does ALSAKKAF HOLDING GROUP convert that architecture into real, controlled, first-month cash — without losing AOS discipline, safety, legality, or cost control?
```

---

# 3. Why This Project Exists

PRJ-001 through PRJ-006 built The Librarian, Atlas, the Markdown Audit Tool, the Data & Deployment Architecture, the Atlas Super Assistant Architecture, and the Partner Factory. That work is foundation, not income.

The Founder now needs to move from building AOS architecture into practical revenue generation, because:

| Pressure | Reason |
|----------|--------|
| Architecture has no cash value until it is applied | AOS has produced governance and Partner infrastructure, not yet a paying client |
| Five business lines cannot launch at once | AI Services, YouTube, Dropshipping, Marketing, and future SaaS each compete for the same limited Founder time and budget |
| Content and ecommerce monetize slowly | YouTube monetization, social growth, and marketplace sales typically take months to produce reliable income |
| Services can monetize fastest | AI Services can be sold directly to a small business client within days using skills AOS already has documented |
| Every revenue action must stay inside AOS discipline | Outreach, pricing, account creation, ad spend, and content publishing all carry legal, financial, and reputational risk if done carelessly |

# 5. Out of Scope

PRJ-007 does not include:

| Out of Scope Item | Reason |
|-------------------|--------|
| Creating real email, social media, website hosting, or marketplace accounts | Account creation is manual and requires direct CEO action, per CLAUDE.md Section 4 and this project's safety rules |
| Sending outreach, publishing content, or creating ads | Requires CEO approval of the specific message, content, or campaign first |
| Spending money or creating paid API usage | Requires a completed budget approval before any spend |
| Activating new Partners or changing Partner Registry status | Partner activation follows the separate Partner Factory lifecycle (PRJ-006, PCL-001) |
| Listing or advertising dropshipping products | Requires CEO product approval per the Dropshipping and Marketplace Experiment plan |
| Guaranteeing any revenue outcome | Revenue figures in this package are cautious planning ranges, not guarantees |
| Building a customer-facing software product | Belongs to a future project once services revenue is proven |

# 6. Revenue First Principle

```text
Revenue comes first from solving real business problems for real clients, not from building endless internal systems.
```

AOS AI Services is the first cash engine because it can generate revenue faster than YouTube monetization, dropshipping, or software SaaS. Content, website, and ecommerce work in this project exist to support lead generation and later revenue — not to replace services as the first month's cash source.

---

# 7. 14-Day Sprint Principle

The first 14 days exist to produce a working, approved offer; a working, approved outreach system; and a first real attempt to close a paying client — using only tools, templates, and manual account actions that stay inside AOS safety rules. The sprint does not require any paid tool, real account creation, or outreach sending to begin; those steps require explicit CEO approval at the point they become necessary, per `AOS_14_Day_Revenue_Sprint.md` (STRAT-006).

---

# 8. Atlas Role

Atlas coordinates the Revenue Launch package: tracking sprint progress, preparing daily/weekly summaries for the CEO, routing security-relevant questions to Guardian, and keeping the Revenue Dashboard data current from approved inputs.

Atlas may research, draft, summarize, score, and recommend across lead generation, content planning, and service delivery preparation.

Atlas may not send outreach, publish content, agree pricing, accept a deal, sign a contract, spend money, or create any real account on its own authority. Every one of those actions requires explicit CEO approval, consistent with CLAUDE.md Section 4 and `AOS_Client_Lead_Pipeline_and_Outreach_System.md` (STRAT-008).

# 10. CEO Approval Gates

The following actions always require explicit CEO approval before they happen, regardless of which document or Partner prepares them:

| Action | Approval Required Before |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Sending any outreach email, DM, or message | Message content and recipient list are approved |
| Publishing any content publicly | Content and platform are approved |
| Creating any ads | Ad content, budget, and platform are approved |
| Creating any real account (email, social, website, marketplace) | Account name, purpose, and security setup are approved |
| Spending any money | A specific amount and purpose are approved |
| Agreeing pricing or closing a deal | Final price and terms are approved |
| Signing any contract | Contract terms are approved |
| Listing or advertising any product | Product, supplier, and compliance review are approved |
| Activating a new Partner | Full Partner Factory lifecycle (PCL-001) is complete |

# 13. Risks

| Risk | Response |
|------|----------|
| Founder tries to run five business lines at once and none gets enough attention | Master Plan explicitly sequences AI Services first, content/website second, ecommerce as a later controlled experiment |
| Outreach sent without review causes reputational or compliance risk | Every outreach batch requires CEO approval per STRAT-008; no automated sending exists |
| Real accounts created carelessly create security or credential exposure | Guardian reviews account security rules before any real account is used, per STRAT-009 |
| Revenue targets are read as guarantees | Master Plan and CEO Briefing state cautious ranges only and explicitly warn against guaranteeing revenue |
| Dropshipping experiment drifts into unapproved product listing or ad spend | STRAT-011 requires CEO approval before any listing, advertising, or spend, with explicit kill/pause/scale gates |
| Content reuse creates copyright or platform risk | STRAT-010 requires original, licensed, or meaningfully transformed content only |
| Package becomes another internal system instead of producing a client | Revenue First Principle and 14-Day Sprint Principle keep the project anchored to a first client outcome |
