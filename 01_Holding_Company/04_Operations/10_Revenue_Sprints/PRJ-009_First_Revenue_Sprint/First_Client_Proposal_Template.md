# 1. Pick the Right Base

| Situation | Use |
|-----------|-----|
| Lead needs systems: lost leads, undocumented processes, no visibility | $399 Workflow Starter Pack — run `atlas.py proposal` for the draft |
| Lead wants AI working in their business daily | $450 AI Agent Starter Pack — run `atlas.py ai-agent-proposal` for the draft, base structure in AIAT-001 |
| Neither fits cleanly | Custom quote — describe scope honestly, price with CEO decision |

# 2. Personalization Pass (mandatory)

For whichever base you use:

- [ ] Their problem, in their words (from the call or their reply — not your assumption)
- [ ] One concrete example per deliverable tied to their business
- [ ] Correct payment path: $399 and $450 each have their own approved PayPal link — included only in the proposal or after a confirmed yes, never in cold outreach
- [ ] Timeline stated from intake answers received, not from payment date
- [ ] The honest-scope paragraph stays in — it closes more deals with serious buyers than it loses

# 3. CEO Final Review Before Sending

- [ ] Every claim is literally true
- [ ] Price, scope, and revision terms match the catalog (STRAT-007)
- [ ] No guarantee language anywhere
- [ ] You would sign this yourself if you were the client

# 4. After Sending

- [ ] Log in `Client_Pipeline.csv`: Service Interested, Quoted Price, Next Action = "Await proposal response, follow up [date]"
- [ ] Follow up once after 3 days if silent, once more after 7 — then park it as future
